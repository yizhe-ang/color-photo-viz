Conceived as Singapore’s first ever satellite town to be planned from scratch by the Housing Development Board (HDB) that was formed in 1960, Toa Payoh was originally home to a large swamp, about 3000 squatters and one of Singapore’s oldest Buddhist Temples, the Lian Shan Shuang Lin that dates back to 1898. .

The town that we know as Toa Payoh today had its first phase of public housing completed in 1966, and more buildings have been added along the way till today. It currently houses over 120,000 people and has some of Singapore’s tallest public housing flats standing at 40 Storeys in height. .
